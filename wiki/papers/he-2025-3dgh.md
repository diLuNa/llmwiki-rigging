## Summary
Generative approach for composable 3D head generation with separate, controllable hair and face components. Enables independent manipulation of facial identity, expression, and hairstyle while maintaining geometric coherence. Supports diverse head morphologies and articulated facial and hair deformations.

## Problem
Existing 3D head generation methods entangle hair and face geometry, preventing independent control over these key appearance components. Separate generation enables flexible avatar customization and style transfer while maintaining rigging compatibility.

## Method
- **Decomposed representation**: Separate learned distributions for face and hair geometry
- **Composable generation**: Independent VAE/diffusion models for face and hair components
- **Shared latent space**: Aligned feature representations enabling cross-component conditioning
- **Deformation learning**: Facial expression and hair dynamics through learned transform operators

## Key Results
- Generates diverse, high-quality 3D head models with independent hair/face control
- Supports expression transfer and hairstyle editing
- Maintains geometric coherence between components
- Produces animation-ready meshes with stable rigging

## Limitations
- Quality depends on training data diversity
- Complex hair styles may require iterative refinement
- Deformation learning limited to seen expression ranges
- Scalp attachment requires careful handling
